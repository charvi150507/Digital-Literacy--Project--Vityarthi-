Case Study: Phishing 

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate trusted organizations to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or OTPs. It is usually carried out through emails, SMS messages, or fake websites that appear legitimate and convincing to users.

A typical phishing attack begins when a student receives a message claiming to be from a bank, university, or popular online service. The message often creates urgency, such as warning about account suspension or suspicious activity. It includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look identical to the official one. The victim is asked to log in or provide personal details like their username, password, or OTP. Once the information is entered, it is captured by the attacker, who can then access the victim’s real account and misuse it.

Students and young adults are commonly targeted because they are highly active online and may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. The consequences of phishing attacks include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal or academic accounts, sometimes causing long-term damage and stress.

This case highlights the importance of staying alert, verifying sources, and avoiding suspicious links, as well as regularly updating passwords and security settings.
